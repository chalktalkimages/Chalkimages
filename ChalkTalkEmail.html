Before the Bell



Equities: Equity markets are higher overnight, as sentiment remains strong following yesterday's rebound. In Europe, the FTSE is unchanged, while the DAX is advancing 0.1% and the CAC is increasing by 0.1%. In Asia, Japan jumped 1.5% and China added 1.1%. Futures markets are pointing to a flattish open in the U.S. (+0.0%) and a more positive one in Canada (+0.2%).

Bonds: Gilts and US Treasuries will be the main sovereign debt markets at risk today ahead of the Brexit vote and US CPI. UK 10s have been very volatile so far today, jumping by as much as 8bps higher before this was reversed when the legal opinion on the Brexit deal was released. The gilts curve is presently slightly dearer especially at the front end. US Ts and Canadas are flat.

Commodities: Commodity prices are higher this morning, with oil (+1.0%) advancing and copper(+1.4%) rising. Gold is up slightly this morning on USD weakness after British Prime Minister May was able to win some last-minute revisions to the Brexit deal with the European Union. European Commission head Jean-Claude Juncker on late Monday evening agreed to a revised Brexit deal to make the agreement more palatable to UK lawmakers. Members of Parliament will be voting on the revised deal this evening. London base metal prices rose across the board in early Asian trade.

Currencies: The greenback is weaker against most other currencies this morning and the DXY is down 0.2% at 97.1. The loonie is weaker relative to the USD, losing 0.1% to US$0.746. Confirmation late last night that UK PM May had secured some adjustment to the Withdrawal Agreement, including additional reassurances on the Irish backstop lifted GBPUSD to near 1.33, pulling the EUR higher and giving the USD a broader push lower. The pound is slipping back as markets remain rightly cautious on the prospect of the new, improved deal making it through parliament but earlier hopes that a hard Brexit can be averted plus broader gains in EM stocks so far this week lifted the risk-taking mood in the markets overall.

Macro Commentary

US Earnings Barometer Pointing to Flattish 2019 Growth: An extremely tight US job market could make it harder for the economy to deliver the kind of job prints investors were used to. Still, workers stand to benefit from the situation. February's payroll report indicated that wage growth hit its fastest pace since the financial crisis at 3.4% YOY and further gains have to be expected. According the NFIB survey released this morning, the net percentage of small business owners reporting higher worker compensation remains near an all-time high, despite a monthly setback. Higher wages should delight Main Street, but corporate America could feel stronger headwinds if it can't grow its top line as fast. Link

Real Estate & REITs- A Positive Start to 2019; Raising Estimates: We are raising our Multifamily FFOPS estimates, NAVPS estimates, and target prices by ~2% on average to reflect positive YTD operations and continued strength in new lease rate growth (3.2% in February, per RealPage). We raise our 2019/20 SSREV growth estimates 25bp on average (to 3.3%/3.6%) and our 2019/20 FFOPS estimates are generally above consensus. Multifamily remains our top subsector (EQR and ESS are preferred picks); we believe Multifamily FFOPS growth will outpace REITs overall in 2019/20 due to accelerating SSREV growth and easing new supply.
