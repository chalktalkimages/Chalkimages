The Halftime Show




Source: Bloomberg Finance LP.

Canadian Market

 

SVC +6.02% , 26.33x ADV
Positive - Francisco Partners reported to be weighing a competing bid for SVC, which is in the middle of the 42 days go-shop period from the C$3.80 bid from Vector Capital announced on May 26. A bump from another PE buyer is possible given solid FCF yield. We would BUY SVC inline for optionality on the go-shop period with potential upside to $4.25+ ($3.90-$3.95 represents an ~25-30% chance we see an alternative offer).

 

HCG +4.06% , 2.66x ADV
Positive - Announced an agreement to sell $1.2B in mortgage assets to KingSett Capital. The proceeds from the transaction are expected to help stabilize the company's liquidity position and reduce the amount drawn under the company's $2Bn credit facility. The initial proceeds to the company will be 97% of the outstanding principal value of the mortgages. The balance of the purchase price will be subject to credit losses in the portfolio. HCG expects to record a loss on the transaction of ~$15M ($0.18/sh after tax) and expects net cash proceeds of ~$1.16B in Q3/17.

 

BBD.B +0.39% , 1.61x ADV
Takeaways from the Air Show: 1) Management remains confident on their ability to meet NT and LT guidance. 2) BBD is seeing signs of life in Europe and US in an environment where most OEMs have now adjusted production down to trough levels. While this is positive, we prefer to remain conservative and are not baking-in any major price increases in our NT forecasts. 3) It has been a while since the last CSeries order. Management, however, remains confident and expects to receive at least one order this year. In our opinion, the ongoing trade dispute with Boeing is a risk in this regard.

 

ERJ -4.02% , 0.00x ADV
Takeaways from Air Show: 1) Commercial order flow slower than expected. We believe that our E-Jet deliveries forecast has some downside risk for 2018. This will likely lead to a YOY decline in EBIT in 2018 with some improvement expected until 2019 as the E2 ramps up. 2) D&S business picking up with order momentum on KC-390. This bodes well for future profitability but likely won't ramp up enough in 2018 to offset the entire decline in E-Jets. 3) Business jets are troughing. ERJ management commented that pricing is more stable and they expect some firming up by the end of the year.

 

ECA -5.64% , 1.67x ADV
Released an operational update on the Permian. Updated type curves across the play and on average, IP rates are up ~20%. Strong well results have led to an increase in premium return inventory in the Permian by 700 locations. ECA now has ~3,500 premium return inventory locations and 12,000 total inventory locations in the Permian. Five-year-plan is to drill less than 30% of the premium locations. ECA holds 3.3B BOE of premium resource potential at $50.00 WTI and $3.00 NYMEX. Key outcomes of 5 year plan includes 300% increase in CF, a doubling of corporate margin and self funding capex.

 

CVE -11.09% , 2.91x ADV
Positive - Ahead of today's investor day, announces CEO Brian Ferguson will retire on October 31st, but stay on in an advisor role until March 2018. 2017 production guidance was updated to 465-498 mboe/d (assuming no dispositions) vs. our original estimate of 466 mboe/d (we assume asset sales of 45 mboe/d in Q4/17). 2017 capex is expected to come in between $1.8-$2B vs. our original estimate $2B. Expected asset sale proceeds of $4-$5B by year-end is above street expectations of $3-$3.5B and is ear marked to pay down the $3.6B asset sale bridge facility.
