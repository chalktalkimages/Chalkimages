Before the Bell



Equities: Global equities are mainly up overnight, ahead of the ECB meeting results and following a surprise deal to extend the US debt ceiling. In Europe, the FTSE is advancing 0.5%, the DAX is adding 1.0%, and the CAC is edging higher by 0.8%. In Asia, Japan was up 0.4%, while China retreated 0.6%, and Australia closed unchanged. Futures are pointing toward a flattish open this morning in the U.S. (+0.0%) and Canada (+0.1%).

Bonds: U.S. 10-year yields are sliding 2bp to 2.08% ahead of the open.

Commodities: Commodity prices are mixed this morning, with copper shedding 0.6% to US$3.13/lb, oil slipping 0.3% to US$48.99/bbl, while gold is rising 0.4% to US$1,344/oz.

Energy Markets: The energy markets are continuing their impressive WTD rally (feels overdone). WTI is up 3.75%, Brent up 2.75% and yet there is a lack of fundamental reason for such strength beyond the damage caused by Tropical Storm Harvey is less than originally feared. Although refineries are back online sooner than expected, the reality is that with the peak summer driving season behind us oil prices shouldn't be trading higher than they were pre-Harvey. With regards to Brent, Libya's largest oil field (Shahara) resumed output yesterday for the first time in two weeks after being shut-in due to the ongoing civil war that should, in theory, result in weaker Brent prices. Headlines remain focused on the unprecedented hurricane season with Harvey now being "replaced" by Hurricane Irma heading towards Florida with winds that have reached 185 mph, while another storm (Jose) has just been upgraded to a hurricane strength and a third (Katia) forming in the Gulf of Mexico.

Currencies: The generally bearish USD undertone and a strengthening bear trend dynamic on the USDCAD chart suggests (very) limited upside scope for the USD in the near-term and we think even minor USD rebounds are a sell, while CAD retains a soft undertone after yesterday's BoC hike. Markets pricing in decent risk of another increase before year end which does not look unreasonable, based on strengthening growth and the BoC's focus on the output gap. Our FV estimate for USDCAD currently stands at 1.2142, suggesting spot is more or less fairly valued, but has some fairly significant overshoot potential to the downside (high 1.18s) even in the very near-term. On the session so far, the EUR is out-performing ahead of today's ECB policy statement while the NZD has resumed its laggard performance. The MXN is modestly firmer on the session, with USD gains well capped under 18 over the past week.

Macro Commentary

Portfolio Strategy - Canadian Dollar and Yields rise on BoC Tightening: The BoC increased its benchmark rate to 1% yesterday (+25 basis points; bp), implementing its second tightening move since last July. Canadian bond yields and C$ have embarked on wild ride in 2017, due to the volatility of the BoC's macro view. After "keeping rate cuts on the table for most of the first half of 2017", the BoC reversed its stance in July. Although investors have quickly embraced the BoC's shift in bias, we would argue that investors may be underestimating US monetary policy. Fed Funds futures are now pricing only one more +25bp hike (futures pricing 2 more hikes/+50bp in Canada). US economic activity is also robust, prices paid have been averaging 60 since the start of the year, and the lagged effects of the weaker US dollar could push inflation higher in coming months. Canadian 10-Yr bond yields are up 22bp to 1.94% YTD while US 10-Yr yields have eased -36bp (2.09%). As yields rise in Canada, and ultimately in the US, TSX Financials stand to benefit, in our view.
