Energy +2.21%, 0.74x ADV

SVY.TO +1.02%, 0.74x ADV - Announced the appointment of Lyle Whitmarsh (currently on the board) to assume the duties of President effective June 1, 2017. Recall, Lyle left Trinidad as its President and CEO on March 12th and has over 33 years of experience in the oil and gas industry. Our thesis on SVY is unchanged and we await the completion of the TOT merger (86% of shares already tendered), which is expected by June 30th.

NBZ.TO +4.69%, 1.22x ADV - Positive: Upgrading to Sector Perform and increasing our PT to $4 from $3.50. We expect new management (post retirement of CEO and COO) to renew company's strategy and garner more attention for their model in the marketplace. In addition, Adam Waterous of Waterous Energy Fund (WEF) will assume one of the four director's seats vacated following the acquisition of the shares from NGP IX and R/C Canada (67% of shares outstanding). Our target price represents a 2018E EV/DACF of 6.4x vs group average of 9.1x, 2018E D/CF of 3.8x and a 2018E effective payout ratio of 125% vs 2.3x and 107% respectively.


Materials +1.36%, 0.88x ADV

RIC.TO -2.73%, 0.56x ADV - Yesterday afternoon we upgraded RIC to sector outperform as we believe the current valuation, exploration upside, and takeover potential provide investors with an attractive entry point into a profitable junior gold producer. RIC currently trades at 0.9x our NAV estimate; a discount from its February price of 1.4x and versus peers at 1.1x. RIC assets provide low cost ounces and exploration upside in a tier one jurisdiction. This may attract a takeover premium from a suitable bidder. Finally, RIC finished Q1 with significant cash and low debt which should help provide expansion upside.

DDC.TO +0.11%, 0.39x ADV - Neutral: Highlighted its exploration program. This release does not change our view ahead of the investor day. The Leslie pipe is the highest priority amongst kimberlites, but is not currently in the mine plan (we ascribe no value for this pipe in our model), but is also low grade pipe with a low diamond price value. For the Kodiak pipe, DDC is planning ground geophysics and a follow-up vertical drill hole in the centre of the pipe in July 2017. Preliminary results are indicative of a moderately diamondiferous kimberlite.


Other News

CUF.UN -0.30%, 0.70x ADV - Negative: Reduced PT to $13.75 from $15 on lower estimates and target multiple (-0.75x to 10x) to reflect a higher risk profile. Following a disappointing Q1, our outlook on CUF remains cautious and growth remains sub-par until 2018. Distribution sustainability remains in question due to a high payout ratio. The ability to maintain an IG rating is also uncertain due to elevated leverage. CUF is trading at 10.4x 2017E AFFO/7% implied cap/16% below NAV. Despite its above-average discount to the sector, we remain sidelined pending more convincing signs of turnaround.

DSG +2.42%, 0.55x ADV - Announced acquisition of ShipRush for US$14M which offers last-mile delivery integration to over 60 different front end marketplaces and shopping carts, shipping, courier, ERP systems, and pulls/consolidates orders from multiple online marketplaces. We think the deal appears consistent with DSG's SMB and e-Commerce strategy, and the latest acquisition helps broaden DSG's reach in this market. This is another move by DSG to provide a logistics solution aimed at the growing Omnichannel trend and to cover the SMB market.

CMG -2.55%, 0.59x ADV - Positive: Q4 beat exceeded us/street due to higher perpetual software licenses. EBITDA and Adj EPS beats were related to the higher recorded revenues. EPS came in at $0.07 vs our $0.06 and consensus of $.07. EBITDA came in at $7.9M vs our $6.9M and consensus of $7.6M. 2017 Maintenance Renewals as reflected in CMG's Deferred Revenue Balance (as at March 31/17) came in at $38.2M vs. SCI $28.0M reflecting a YOY increase of 14% (vs SCI expectations of -17%).

LB -2.46%, 5.77x ADV - Restricted. Announced the acquisition of Northpoint Commercial Finance through a subsidiary. Northpoint is an inventory finance lender with an equipment finance portfolio of ~$1.2B and operations in the US and Canada. LB cited Northpoint's scale as it pertains to manufacturers and their dealer networks as a reason for the acquisition. LB expects the acquisition to be accretive to EPS in its first full year of operations, and by ~4% in 2019. LB plans to finance the acquisition through a $200M bought deal equity offering of subscription receipts priced at $51.70, plus ~$25M to the Caisse.

QBR.B +0.87%, 0.85x ADV - Positive: Raised our PT to $50 from $48 on higher wireless margins reflecting scale. We are now valuing wireless separately as it is cash flow positive for the first time. By placing a 7x EBITDA multiple on core cable (discount vs. peers), we estimate the wireless business is trading at ~11x NTM EBITDA which on a per-subscriber basis is valued at a ~30% discount vs. the incumbent avg, which we think will shrink as QBR's ARPU gap narrows. In our target NAV, we used 13x forward wireless EBITDA which is equivalent to a 10% discount vs. the incumbents' persubscriber target wireless valuation.

WBA +0.00%, 0.00x ADV - WBA shares were off 3.3% Wed, in part on reports that AMZN is considering entering into the pharma distribution business. While we would never be completely dismissive of a potential AMZN threat, we think that fears may be overblown because: (1) online delivery of scripts is already well established (~20% share), (2) pharmacy distribution is complex, and (3) scale advantages for the largest players (WBA / CVS) could be hard to beat. We view this industry as one that will prove more difficult to disrupt and, as such, do not perceive these efforts as near/medium term risk for WBA.
