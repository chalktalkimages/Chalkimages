Macro Commentary

Initiating Coverage of Two US Healthcare REITs: The two Healthcare REITs focus on the Skilled Nursing vertical: Omega Healthcare Investors, Inc. (OHI) and Sabra Health Care REIT, Inc. (SBRA). The Skilled Nursing industry has experienced both regulatory pressures and payment model changes in recent years, and there is some optimism that its lows are behind it, with baseline expectations having been reset. An ageing U.S. population is likely to result in higher demand for skilled nursing beds in the coming years, while payment models to be implemented in 2019 are expected to create cost savings. However, we caution that occupancy, quality-mix, and wage growth data suggest continued pressure on operating results in the near-term. In the Healthcare subsector, we continue to prefer HCP, Inc. (HCP), as we favor its higher exposure to Life Science and Medical Office versus Seniors Housing and Skilled Nursing.

Energy Infrastructure - Cautious Q4 Preview: A strong start to 2019 for the equities, but we caution Q4 results could be a little soft, especially for mid-streamers (commodity price headwinds). We are generally below street for midstreamers by 2-4% (IPL, KEY, PPL), although we see modest potential beats from ENB (~3% above) and EMA (~5% above). We maintain a defensive bias: ENB remains our preferred name. We expect investors will largely look through Enbridge's and TransCanada's results and instead focus on the outlooks for their major projects. PPL is still our favourite midstreamer, while we think ALA deserves a look given valuation discount (~3x on P/E) and improved funding visibility. AQN is our top utility, while we continue to like TA to play the Alberta power market. Link

Real Estate & REITs: CBRE released its Q4/18 Canadian Cap Rate & Investment Insights. Bottom-line, Toronto/Quebec City Apartments and Montreal Industrial saw further cap rate compression . We see the potential for higher NAVs coming out of Q4/18 results, particularly for Toronto-centric portfolios . The estimated national avg. fell 2bp and 7bp q/q and y/y to 6.05% vs. a 46bp decline in 10YR GoC bond yield and 14bp increase in REIT implied cap rate. REITs have made up for lost time in 2019 however, with the avg. implied cap rate falling 21bp. The strong start has compressed the gap between REIT implied cap rate (6.0%) and private market cap rate (5.6%) to 40bp, below the 60bp avg. since 2005; min/max = 10bp/190bp. We highlight that REIT unit prices tend to lead private market cap rates by ~6 months (highest correlation), implying potential modest additional downside in select private market cap rates, particularly given apparent strength in Canadian CRE lending appetite.
